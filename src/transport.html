Duffield has fairly restricted car parking space. Here are the spaces designated for "disabled parking"

  • Eyes Meadow
  • Viceroy restaurant, Tamworth Street
  • Chapel Street has end-on parking that is more suitable for rear access to vehicles

Duffield train station is disabled "unfriendly" with platform access only over a metal footbridge.

East Midlands Railway will arrange transportation to the nearest accessible station to you and arrange assistance to support you on to and off the train. Contact East Midlands Railway on 03457 125678 or email contact@eastmidlandsrailway.co.uk

The new Passenger Assistance App by Transreport allows you to:

  • request assistance for your rail journey
  • manage your customer profile
  • view your travel history
  • browse rail journeys via the online journey planner

Ecclesbourne Valley Railway has level access.

Buses are frequent in Town Street travelling along the A6.

Trent Barton buses on the sixes and across the network are wheelchair-friendly. The team will always do everything reasonably practicable to carry all customers. This includes encouraging, where possible, those occupying areas designed to assist wheelchair users, to vacate them.

Wheelchair or scooter - if it fits, it’s allowed on. Only one wheelchair user the bus at any one time and they must be positioned with the back against the specially designed upright cushion in order to travel safely.

A vast majority of buses are fitted with audio and visual announcements helping to ensure people with sight loss can confidently and competently travel self-reliantly.
